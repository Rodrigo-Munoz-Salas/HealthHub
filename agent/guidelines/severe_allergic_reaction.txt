Severe Allergic Reaction Emergency Guidelines

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Call 911 immediately
2. Check for epinephrine auto-injector
3. Help person use epinephrine if available
4. Monitor breathing continuously
5. Position person appropriately

WARNING SIGNS:
- Difficulty breathing or wheezing
- Swelling of face, lips, or throat
- Hives or widespread rash
- Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea
- Dizziness or fainting
- Rapid or weak pulse

EMERGENCY LEVEL: CRITICAL
- Severe allergic reaction can be fatal within minutes
- Anaphylaxis requires immediate treatment
- Time is critical
- Call 911 immediately

PREVENTION:
- Identify and avoid known allergens
- Carry epinephrine auto-injector if prescribed
- Wear medical alert bracelet
- Inform others about allergies
- Regular medical checkups
- Have emergency action plan
