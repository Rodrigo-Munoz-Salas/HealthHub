Seizure Emergency Guidelines

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Call 911 immediately
2. Do not restrain the person
3. Clear area of dangerous objects
4. Place person on their side
5. Time the seizure duration

WARNING SIGNS:
- Loss of consciousness
- Convulsions or jerking movements
- Loss of bladder or bowel control
- Biting tongue or lips
- Difficulty breathing
- Seizure lasting more than 5 minutes

EMERGENCY LEVEL: CRITICAL
- Seizures can be life-threatening
- Status epilepticus requires immediate treatment
- Risk of injury during seizure
- Call 911 immediately

PREVENTION:
- Take prescribed medications regularly
- Get adequate sleep
- Avoid seizure triggers
- Regular medical checkups
- Wear medical alert bracelet
- Avoid alcohol and drugs
