Severe Nausea and Vomiting Guidelines

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Help person sit up or turn on side
2. Provide small sips of water if tolerated
3. Monitor for signs of dehydration
4. Call 911 if severe or persistent
5. Check for signs of poisoning

WARNING SIGNS:
- Vomiting blood or dark material
- Severe abdominal pain with vomiting
- Signs of dehydration (dry mouth, no urination)
- Vomiting with severe headache
- Vomiting with stiff neck
- Persistent vomiting for more than 24 hours

EMERGENCY LEVEL: HIGH
- Severe vomiting can cause dehydration
- May indicate serious underlying condition
- Risk of aspiration if unconscious
- Seek medical attention if severe

PREVENTION:
- Practice good food hygiene
- Avoid contaminated food
- Stay hydrated
- Eat small, frequent meals
- Manage stress levels
- Regular medical checkups
