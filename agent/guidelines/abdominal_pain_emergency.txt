Abdominal Pain Emergency Guidelines

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Assess pain severity and location
2. Check for signs of shock
3. Do not give food or water if severe
4. Call 911 for severe or worsening pain
5. Monitor vital signs continuously

WARNING SIGNS:
- Severe, sudden onset pain
- Pain with fever and vomiting
- Pain in lower right abdomen (appendicitis)
- Blood in vomit or stool
- Severe tenderness when touched
- Signs of shock (pale, cold, rapid pulse)

EMERGENCY LEVEL: HIGH
- Severe abdominal pain can indicate life-threatening conditions
- Appendicitis, bowel obstruction, or internal bleeding
- Call 911 immediately for severe pain

PREVENTION:
- Maintain regular bowel habits
- Stay hydrated
- Eat a balanced diet
- Avoid overeating
- Regular medical checkups
- Report persistent digestive issues
